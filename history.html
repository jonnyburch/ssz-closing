We've put thousands of hours into ShellsuitZombie over the last 8 years, with approaching 900 blog posts, 20-30 events and 6 issues of our print magazine. Here's an incredibly brief rundown of what happened each year.



2009

Early days of ShellsuitZombie

The year it all began. Jonny hacked at wordpress to set up a blog, we wandered up to D&AD and drunkenly asked them to help us get to graduates. What followed was a stand at the D&AD New Blood show that year, a poster (we couldn't afford to print a magazine) and a couple of parties. Rock and Roll.

Standout moments: getting 100 students to block the main walkway at New Blood, working with Spin on a poster, meeting the cool cats at D&AD, getting a blog working.



2010

We came into 2010 swinging, with a party at the Old Blue Last in Shoreditch (in which we discovered how popular a dick drawing contest can be) and a bunch of visits to universities (Leeds, Liverpool etc.) to talk graduating.

Drunk with the power bestowed by our own rickshaw, we tore up New Blood 2010.

New Blood that years was a much bigger affair too - we bought a photocopier and ran workshops with agencies and brands as well as a wicked magazine-making workshop with Feverzine.

Most importantly, we printed our first ever issue of the ShellsuitZombie magazine, which we sold for £2.50 on our website. It was in black and white with a pull out Eboy poster, and it had a lot of swearing in it.
